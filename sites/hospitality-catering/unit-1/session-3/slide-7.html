Media

The term ‘media’ is used to describe any way in which news and information is communicated to others. This can include:

  • Newspapers and magazines
  • Television news broadcasts
  • Television programmes
  • Television advertising
  • Social media websites
  • Advertising posters/billboards

The information that we see, hear and read in the media can have a great impact on the choices we make and the opinions we have. For this reason, if positive information about a hospitality or catering organisation is presented in the media, it will build a positive reputation with customers (and potential customers).

However, if negative information about an organisation is shared through the media, then this can damage the reputation of the organisation and lead to a loss of customers.

The growing use of social media sites means that it is now very easy for customers to share information about organisations. They can use social media websites to share positive and negative reviews quickly and easily with thousands of other users. The impact of social media is such that organisations can very quickly gain a positive or negative reputation, based entirely what customers decide to post and share with their friends online!
