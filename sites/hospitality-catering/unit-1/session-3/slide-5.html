Customer service

Working in the hospitality and catering industry is all about serving customers and making sure that their needs are met. The type of service that is provided to customers will determine the success of the organisation – if good customer service is provided, then customers will be happy and continue to use the organisation’s services; if bad customer service is provided, then customers may avoid using the organisation in future and they may also tell friends and family members to do the same.

As you learned on the previous page, customers can have a wide range of expectations and needs. Customer satisfaction is achieved by meeting these needs and expectations.

Features of good customer service

Employees in hospitality/catering organisations can display the following behaviours in order to provide good customer service:

  • Friendly and welcoming
  • Remembering a customer’s name and using it when talking to them
  • Being polite and respectful
  • Making eye contact when talking to customers
  • Being aware of a customer’s likes and dislikes, and adapting service to suit these preferences
  • Having good knowledge of the organisation’s products/services
  • Doing their very best to be helpful and to ensure customers have everything they need
  • Making the customer feel valued
  • Making the customer want to return to the organisation again in the future
