Customer needs and expectations

Customer needs relate to the specific requirements that a customer has when visiting a hospitality/catering establishment. These needs will determine the choices that customers make when deciding on which establishment to visit.

For example, if a customer wanted to meet a friend for lunch during their working day, they may have the following needs:

  • Quick food service
  • Establishment to be located near to their place of work
  • Quiet atmosphere so they can chat easily with their friend

In addition to these needs, customers will also have particular expectations around the experience they will have when visiting the hospitality/catering establishment – these can be realistic or unrealistic!

For example, the customer meeting their friend for lunch may have expectations that their chosen establishment will:

  • Be clean and tidy
  • Have few customers so they don’t have to queue or wait too long for food
  • Have lots of tables to choose from
  • Have lots of choice on the menu
  • Serve great food
  • Have polite, friendly staff

  • Leisure customers

    Leisure customers may have the following needs/expectations when visiting a hospitality/catering establishment:

    • Good value for money
    • Good customer service
    • Menus offering a wide choice of dishes
    • Menus offering dishes that cater for specific dietary needs – such as vegetarian/vegan options, gluten free options, information on ingredients that could cause allergic reactions, etc – and for these options to be clearly indicated on the menu
    • Clean, well-maintained facilities (toilets, changing facilities, etc)
    • Families are likely to want children’s menus, play areas, high chairs, easy access for prams/pushchairs, crayons and activity sheets, baby changing facilities, etc
    • Tourists may want information on attractions/the local area, local food, the option to buy souvenirs, postcards, etc
    • Older customers may need easy access to the establishment and a lift available if they need to access facilities above the ground floor level

  • Business customers’ requirements

    Business customers may have the following needs/expectations when visiting a hospitality/catering establishment:

    • Meeting rooms
    • Special rates for business customers
    • Water/juice available in meeting rooms
    • Stationery (notepads, pens, etc) available for meetings
    • Access to a projector and/or television if they are giving a presentation
    • Access to office facilities, such as a printer, telephone and internet access
    • Tea, coffee and biscuits for mid-morning/mid-afternoon breaks
    • Meals to be provided (this could be breakfast, lunch and/or dinner, depending on the nature of the business event)
    • Accommodation may be needed, if customers are travelling from a long distance away
    • Food to be served quickly if time is limited (for example, business customers may only have an hour available to attend a lunch meeting)

  • Local customers’ requirements

    Local customers may have the following needs/expectations when visiting a hospitality/catering establishment:

    • To be recognised and welcomed by staff
    • Equally good standard of service every time they visit
    • Good value for money
    • Loyalty schemes
    • Menu specials – such as ‘early bird’ offers, happy hours, mid-week specials, etc
    • Varied entertainment options
    • Mailing list for special offers, vouchers, discounts, etc
