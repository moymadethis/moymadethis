Customer types

Preparing for your assessment

In your assessment for this Unit, you will need to be able to demonstrate that you are able to:

  • Explain how hospitality and catering provision meet customer requirements

You’ll find lots of useful information over the following pages to help you complete this part of your assessment.

If a business wants to meet customer requirements, it must first understand what these requirements are! There are many different types of hospitality and catering business (commercial, non-commercial, residential, non-residential), and there are many different types of customer.

Customer types may be broken down into the following three categories:

  1. Leisure
  2. Business/corporate
  3. Local residents

Leisure customers

These customers will visit hospitality/catering establishments in their leisure time – for example, to have a meal with friends, to have a family day out, to visit tourist attractions, etc.

Business/corporate customers

These customers will use hospitality/catering establishments for business related activities – for example, business meetings, lunches/dinners with customers, presentations, conferences, training courses, etc.

Local residents

These are customers who live near an establishment and visit it on a regular basis – for example, going to a local restaurant for Sunday lunch every week or buying a takeaway meal every Saturday evening.
