Customer trends

As you learned in Session 3 of this Unit, customer trends and preferences can have a great impact on hospitality/catering establishments.

Customer preferences can be influenced by many factors, including:

  • Marketing/advertising – for example, on television, radio, the internet, etc
  • Media content (such as newspaper/magazine articles, social media posts, etc)
  • Customer reviews and ratings
  • Health factors
  • Travel experiences

It is important for hospitality/catering establishments to consider customer trends in the service they provide – if a business can offer a product or service that is very popular, then it has the potential to appeal to a large number of customers and increase sales. This can also give a business an advantage over its competitors – for example, if a local café is the only establishment in a town that serves vegan food, then other businesses in the area may lose customers to this café.

Some examples of customer trends that businesses may choose to cater for include:

  • Street food
  • Organic food
  • Food and drink containing less sugar
  • Clean eating
