Treating customers equally

Businesses have a legal requirement to treat every customer equally. The Equality Act 2010 is the key piece of legislation that businesses must be compliant with.

The Equality Act 2010 sets out to protect individuals from discrimination. There are nine protected characteristics in the Equality Act – these are:

Glossary

Discrimination
Discrimination means that you are being treated unfairly because of who you are.
  1. Age
  2. Disability
  3. Gender or gender reassignment
  4. Marriage or civil partnership
  5. Pregnancy and maternity
  6. Race
  7. Religion or belief
  8. Sex
  9. Sexual orientation

All hospitality/catering businesses must adhere to this legislation and treat all customers equally. It is illegal for businesses to serve or refuse to serve a customer based on any of the nine protected characteristics.

Examples

A hotel could provide the following services/facilities to accommodate wheelchair bound customers:

  • A ramp to access the hotel, instead of steps
  • Rooms that allow enough space for free movement of a wheelchair
  • Hand rails to aid with movement in the bathroom
  • A wheel-in shower or wet room
  • Wider doorways to allow space for wheelchairs to pass through
  • Disabled toilets on the ground floor of the hotel
  • Easy lift access to other floors
  • Alarms in bedrooms and disabled toilets, in case customers need to call for assistance
  • Designated staff members who would assist disabled customers in the case of an emergency (such as the hotel being evacuated)
