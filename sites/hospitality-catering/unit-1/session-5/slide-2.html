Front of house operations

Preparing for your assessment

In your assessment for this Unit, you will need to be able to demonstrate that you are able to:

  • Describe the operation of front of house

You’ll find lots of useful information over the following pages to help you complete this part of your assessment.

Think first

Do you know what is meant by the term ‘front of house’? Have a go at typing a definition into the box below before you move on.

In hospitality and catering, the term ‘front of house’ refers to all of the activities and operations that a customer sees and experiences during their visit to an establishment – for example, think of a restaurant and all of the activities you see when visiting one (such as someone welcoming you, showing you to your table, taking your order, serving your food, etc) – these are all front of house activities.

In the previous Session, you learned all about kitchen operations – now let’s consider all of the front of house operations that link the kitchen operations with the end customer.

As you have learned already during this Unit, there are many different types of food/drink services within the hospitality and catering industry. The front of house operations in a food/drink establishment will vary depending on the type of service that is taking place – for example, a fast food restaurant will have different front of house operations to a hotel bar and restaurant.
