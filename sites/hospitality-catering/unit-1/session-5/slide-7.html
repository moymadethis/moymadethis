Stock control

As with kitchen operations, stock control is a very important part of front of house operations – sufficient stock in all areas is essential for the smooth running of the operation.

For example, consider the issues that a restaurant would face if it ran out of wine, or didn’t have enough knives and forks for customers to use!

Some examples of stock that would be used during front of house operations include:

  • Wines
  • Spirits
  • Soft drinks
  • Straws
  • Coffee
  • Tea
  • Order pads
  • Cutlery
  • Crockery
  • Disposable table linen (such as paper napkins)
  • Snacks for bar area and/or restaurant tables (nuts, olives, breadsticks, etc)
  • Other consumables (salt, pepper, ketchup, etc)

There must be a clear stock control process in place – this process will ensure that stock is effectively recorded, checked and re-ordered when necessary. Stock must be ordered before it is actually needed, to allow for processing and delivery time.

For any items of stock that are perishable, front of house must operate a ‘first in, first out’ system – this means that the earliest received stock is used first, while subsequent deliveries are stored for use later on.
