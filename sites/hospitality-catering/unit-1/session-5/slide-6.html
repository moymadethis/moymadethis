Equipment and materials

The equipment that is needed for front of house operations can generally be divided into two categories – hand equipment and powered equipment.

Hand equipment

Hand equipment includes the following items:

  • Crockery (plates, dishes, etc)
  • Cutlery (knives, forks, spoons, etc)
  • Table linen (napkins, table cloths, etc)
  • Glassware
  • Serving equipment
  • Tables, chairs and sideboards

The type of hand equipment that is used will depend upon the type of menu/service offered, cost and availability of washing up facilities. For example, a fast food restaurant may serve food in disposable packaging and have easy to clean tables. Meanwhile, a fine dining restaurant may use china crockery, silver cutlery, crystal glasses and high quality table linen.

Some key points to consider when using hand equipment include:

  • Cutlery (and crockery where possible) should be dishwasher proof.
  • Cutlery should be stored carefully to avoid scratches and marks. It should also be covered to prevent contact with germs or dust.
  • Glassware should be washed, stored and handled carefully to avoid breakages.
  • It’s important to bear in mind that plain cutlery is easier to clean than patterned cutlery.
  • Stainless steel cutlery is a good option as it resists scratches, meaning that it looks newer for longer.
  • Table linen should be washed, folded and then stored in cupboards/drawers, away from dust.

Powered equipment

Examples of powered equipment that may be used in front of house operations include:

  • Hand-held credit or debit card machines
  • Coffee machines
  • Toasters
  • Hot and cold service counters
  • Electronic Point of Sale (EPOS) systems, which can be used to send orders through from waiting staff to the kitchen staff

Whenever electrical equipment is used, it must undergo a safety check every year. Equipment that customers can see must also be kept clean at all times (dirty equipment will give a bad impression to customers about hygienic the establishment is).
