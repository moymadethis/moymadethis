Dress code

The dress code in front of house operations is very focused on creating a positive first impression to customers. Many establishments ask staff to wear a uniform to ensure that all front of house staff are smartly presented and also easily identifiable to customers (and other staff members). The uniform may change slightly across different job roles â€“ for example, the Restaurant Manager and Head Waiter/Waitress may wear a slightly different uniform to other waiting staff.

As well as having set uniform guidelines, an establishment is likely to have certain standards of personal presentation that it expects all staff to follow. For example:

  • Always wear a clean, smart uniform
  • Maintain good standards of personal hygiene
  • Keep jewellery to a minimum
  • Wear natural makeup and light perfume
  • Keep hair clean and wear long hair tied back

Any member of staff working in a front of house role needs to be able to give customers a positive impression, both in the way they present themselves and the way they behave. Creating and giving a positive impression to customers is vital for a business to succeed. If customers form a good first impression of a business based on staff appearance, this can contribute to improved levels of customer satisfaction which in turn can lead to increased customer spending, customer loyalty and repeat business.

Examples of positive staff behaviours include:

  • Using effective communication and listening skills when interacting with customers
  • Showing patience and consideration when dealing with customer issues
  • Using positive body language (smiling, making eye contact, etc)
  • Being courteous and polite
  • Having a positive and helpful attitude

Key point

The appearance of the establishment also plays an important part in creating a positive image to the customer. For example, what impression would you get from a restaurant with worn out carpets, dirty toilets and cutlery covered in fingerprints? Now, compare this with how you would feel in a restaurant that was very well presented, with sparkling glasses on the table, clean toilets and polished floors!
