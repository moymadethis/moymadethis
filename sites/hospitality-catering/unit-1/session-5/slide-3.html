As you learned in Session 1, food can be served in many ways. The type of service depends on the following factors:

  • The type of establishment or where it is
  • The type of food or menu being served
  • The cost of the meal or food
  • The time available for the meal
  • The type of customer
  • The number of customers expected
  • The availability of skilled serving staff

Key point

The front of house layout will depend on the type of food being served and the type of service offered. For example, a large restaurant will need a layout that accommodates lots of tables, perhaps a bar area and an entrance area for customers to be greeted; alternatively, a buffet restaurant will need lots of counter space for displaying food and enough space around these counters for customers to queue and select their food.

The key to effective layout is to make sure that customers are offered the best possible experience – this will improve levels of customer satisfaction and increase repeat business/customer loyalty…they will want to keep coming back to the establishment to repeat an enjoyable experience.

Think about some of the different food and drink establishments that you have visited. How do their layouts differ? Are there any common features in the layout of different types of establishment? For example, they will all need an area where customers can make payment for their food/drink.
