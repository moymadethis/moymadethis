Risks to personal security

Workers in the hospitality and catering industry may also be exposed to situations that present a risk to their personal security.

Think First

Can you think of any situations that could present a risk to personal security in hospitality or catering roles? Type some ideas into the box below before you move on.

Risks to personal security may arise when dealing with:

  • Disagreements between customers
  • Customers who have been drinking alcohol or taking drugs
  • Customers who are verbally abusive
  • Customers who are physically abusive
  • Large amounts of money or high value items in open areas
  • Customer complaints

In particular, young workers with limited experience, workers responsible for opening or locking up the establishment and night shift workers/lone workers who have fewer people around them may be most at risk in these situations.

Risks to personal security can be minimised by:

  • Making sure outside areas are well lit and perhaps have CCTV cameras installed
  • Make senior staff members responsible for high risk tasks, such as dealing with difficult/abusive customers, handling cash and opening/locking up
  • Offering training to staff on how to handle difficult and abusive customers
  • Making sure lone workers know what to do and who to contact in an emergency
