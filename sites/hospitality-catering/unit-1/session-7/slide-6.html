Personal safety control measures

Preparing for your assessment

In your assessment for this Unit, you will need to be able to demonstrate that you are able to:

  • Recommend personal safety control measures for hospitality and catering provision

You’ll find lots of useful information on this page to help you complete this part of your assessment.

Control measures are actions that can be taken to remove a hazard or reduce the likelihood of risk being caused by a hazard. We have covered a wide range of control measures already in this Session – for example:

  • Storing chemicals safely and securely
  • Providing protective equipment for staff carrying out hazardous tasks
  • Providing appropriate training and information to reduce the potential for harm (for example, correct lifting techniques for manual handling operations, training on safe use of knives in the kitchen, etc)
  • Keeping flammable materials separate from heat sources, to reduce the risk of fire
  • Reporting of accidents and dangerous occurrences
  • Keeping work areas clean and clear from obstructions

Control measures are essential in ensuring health and safety of both employees and customers. Take a look at the table below to see some examples of control measures and the benefit they can have for employees and/or customers.

Control measure Benefits for employees Benefits for customers
Keeping work areas clean and clear from obstructions
  • Clean work areas allow for more effective and efficient working
  • Work environment is safer for the employees
  • Areas clear from obstructions present a reduced risk of trips and falls
  • Clean work areas in a kitchen will reduce the risk of food becoming contaminated during the cooking process, which reduces the risk of food poisoning
  • When public areas of the organisation are kept clear from obstructions, it reduces the risk of customers suffering trips and falls
Keeping flammable materials separate from heat sources
  • Reduced risk of fire causing damage, injury and potentially death
  • Reduced risk of fire causing damage, injury and potentially death
Providing training on correct lifting techniques for manual handling operations
  • Reduced risk of injury when performing manual handling operations
  • As there is a reduced risk of injury to employees, this means that staffing levels in the organisation will not be affected and customer service levels are maintained (consider the impact if the organisation had members of staff away from work due to manual handling injuries – they may not have sufficient staff to cover certain areas of service and to meet customer expectations)
Storing chemicals safely and securely
  • Reduced risk of harm to employees due to potentially dangerous chemicals/substances being handled correctly and only accessed/used by those employees specially trained to do so
  • In catering organisations, the risk of chemical contamination of food is reduced

Control measures for customer safety

  • Keeping customer areas clean and clear, to avoid trips and slips
  • Clearing up any spills in customer areas immediately
  • Warning customers if plates are hot when food is served
  • Regularly checking and maintaining electrical equipment that will be used by customers (e.g. hand dryers, hair dryers and kettles in hotel rooms, etc)
  • Ensuring that car parks and walkways are well lit at night
  • Using clear warning/caution signs when cleaning is taking place, to indicate potentially slippery surfaces
  • Ensuring that any maintenance areas are clearly marked and customers are not permitted access to these areas
  • Using signs to warn customers of potential hazards – for example, “Mind your head”, “Watch your step”, “Very hot water”, etc
