Suppliers to the hospitality and catering industry

Later in this Session, you will learn about the different types of jobs that are available within the hospitality and catering industry.

The hospitality and catering industry also supports employment across other types of organisations and industries as it relies on them to act as suppliers (providing the resources that it needs to operate effectively).

Activity

Consider the following two hospitality and catering businesses:

  1. A fish and chip shop
  2. A large hotel

For each business, think about the different organisations it may rely upon to supply goods and services. Make some notes in the box below before you move on.

Now click on the buttons below to find out more.

  • Fish & Chip Shop
  • A Large Hotel

A Fish & Chip Shop

This business may rely on the following suppliers:

  • Food suppliers to provide fish, potatoes and other ingredients (along with salt, vinegar, ketchup, soft drinks, etc)
  • A packaging supplier to provide cartons to serve the food in
  • Perhaps a company to supply uniforms with the company name embroidered on
  • Maintenance company to clean and repair the fryers that are used to cook the fish and chips

A Large Hotel

The hotel may rely on the following suppliers:

  • A laundry company to clean the hotel bedding, towels, tablecloths, napkins, etc
  • Food suppliers or specialist markets to provide ingredients for the hotel restaurants
  • A company to supply alcoholic and non-alcoholic drinks to serve in the hotel bars and restaurants
  • It may use a local maintenance company to perform ongoing repairs and refurbishments in the hotel
  • A company to supply branded stationery for the hotel (for use in hotel bedrooms and any conference facilities)
