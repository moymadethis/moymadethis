The hospitality and catering industry

Preparing for your assessment

In your assessment for this Unit, you will need to be able to demonstrate that you are able to:

  • Describe the structure of the hospitality and catering industry

You’ll find lots of useful information over the following pages to help you complete this part of your assessment.

Think first

The hospitality and catering industry is one of the largest industries in the UK and you can see examples of hospitality and catering all around you. Think about the town where you live – how many examples of hospitality and catering can you identify? List as many examples as you can in the box below before you move on.

The term hospitality is used to describe a relationship between a guest and a host; therefore, the hospitality industry is made up of all organisations that welcome, entertain and provide services for guests – for example, hotels, theme parks, restaurants, etc.

Within these hospitality organisations, there may also be a need to provide catering for guests – catering is all about providing food services to customers. This can range from pre-packed sandwiches in a small café to fine dining at large functions such as weddings.

Hospitality and catering examples

The hospitality and catering industry includes:

  • Hotels
  • Guest houses
  • Bed and breakfasts (B&Bs)
  • Self-catering accommodation
  • Youth hostels
  • University accommodation (halls of residence)
  • Public houses (pubs) and bars
  • Nightclubs
  • Casinos
  • Restaurants
  • Coffee shops
  • Cafes
  • Cruise ships
  • Takeaways and fast food outlets
  • Motorway service stations
  • Catering services provided on transport services (such as trains and ferries)
  • Catering services provided in tourist attractions (such as museums, theme parks, etc)
  • Catering services provided in leisure facilities (such as swimming pools, ice rinks, bowling alleys, cinemas, etc)
  • Mobile catering services such as ice cream vans or food stalls (often used at outdoor events or festivals)
  • Catering provided in the service sector (such as in hospitals, schools and prisons)
