Standards and ratings

With so many hospitality and catering businesses available, it can be difficult to know which one to choose! Fortunately, there are a range of hospitality standards and ratings that can help to indicate the quality and service you can expect from each organisation.

In 2006, the AA developed a set of Common Quality Standards, designed to be used when inspecting and rating accommodation services. These standards are used to rate accommodation providers throughout the UK, providing a guide for customers on the type of service and quality they can expect. Inspectors visit restaurants and hotels, write a review and award an appropriate rating based on their experience.

Depending on the type of accommodation, either a star rating or pennant rating is given. Star ratings are given to hotels, guest houses, self-catering accommodation and serviced accommodation, while pennant ratings are given to caravan and camping sites.

Restaurants are awarded rosettes for culinary excellence.

While the AA ratings can offer a guide on standards of all UK-based businesses, the Michelin star rating system is used as a global standard to indicate hotels and restaurants offering a certain level of quality. Key point

There are now also a wide range of online review sites that customers can use to give ratings and feedback on restaurants, hotels, etc. For example:

  • www.tripadvisor.co.uk
  • www.opentable.co.uk

Customers may be asked to rate the business on factors such as quality of food, menu choice, level of service, atmosphere, price and overall experience.

There are also food critics who may write articles in newspapers and magazines, based on their dining experience in a restaurant.
