Outside catering

Think first

Have you heard the term ‘outside catering’ before? What do you think is meant by this term? Type some ideas into the box below before you move on.

Outside catering involves a specialist catering organisation (known as ‘contract caterers’) offering food and drinks service at venues that do not specialise in catering itself (or where the venue is unable to cater for the amount of guests expected). The food is usually prepared elsewhere and then served at the event.

For example, outside catering may be used to cater for:

  • Sporting events, such as motor racing, rugby matches, etc
  • Weddings, business lunches, parties and other functions held at venues without catering facilities (such as offices, private homes, etc)
  • Outdoor festivals

As well as providing food and drinks, the catering organisation may also be able to provide additional services including linen (napkins, tablecloths, etc), cutlery, glasses, tables, chairs and staff, all depending on the nature of the event and the customer’s needs.
