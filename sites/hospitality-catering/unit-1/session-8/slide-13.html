Risk assessment

Another effective way of ensuring compliance with food safety legislation in the cooking environment is to carry out a risk assessment.

What is a risk assessment?

A risk assessment is a way of identifying anything that might cause harm to people and deciding whether enough is being done to minimise/prevent this risk of harm. Risk assessments help you to focus particularly on risks that have potential to cause real harm and put measures in place to control these risks.

You can begin carrying out a risk assessment in your cooking environment by simply looking around and making a note of any hazards (things that may cause harm). Then, think about the risk of someone being harmed by each hazard ...is it high or low, and how serious could the harm be? Also, think about how accidents could happen and who might be harmed. In a hospitality and catering business, there are five steps involved in carrying out a risk assessment:

  1. Identify the hazard
  2. Decide who might be harmed and how
  3. Evaluate the risks and decide on precautions
  4. Record the findings and implement them
  5. Review the assessment and update regularly
