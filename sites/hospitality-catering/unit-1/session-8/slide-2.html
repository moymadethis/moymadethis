Safe and hygienic working practices

Preparing for your assessment

Think first

Before we begin, spend a few minutes thinking about safety and hygiene in the kitchen (or any other catering service). See if you can list a few examples of actions that should always be taken to ensure the cooking/catering environment is safe and hygienic.

In any catering organisation, food preparation work and cooking must be carried out in an area that is safe and hygienic. This includes members of staff always having good personal hygiene, such as:

  • Wearing an apron
  • Washing their hands and nails
  • Tying long hair back or covering it so it doesn’t go into food
  • Removing jewellery
  • Not touching their hair or face
  • Sneezing and coughing away from food

Personal hygiene is very important because good hygiene prevents food poisoning and the spread of illnesses such as coughs and colds. Dirty hands in particular can spread bacteria, therefore effective hand washing is essential and it should be done:

  • Before handling food
  • After handling raw food or packaging of raw food
  • After using the toilet
  • After touching bins or waste
  • After blowing your nose
  • After eating or drinking
  • After touching your ears, nose and/or mouth

Key point

Some types of bacteria need to enter your body through the food that you eat before they can cause you to feel ill. Other types of bacteria produce a toxin (poison) on the food, which then makes you ill when you eat that food.

As well as good personal hygiene, it is also important to make sure that the cooking environment is clean and safe to cook in.

Every time that catering staff prepare and cook food, they must make sure that they have prepared the work area so that it is safe and hygienic. Click on the buttons below to learn more about the importance of a safe and clean cooking environment.

  • Clean cooking environment
  • Safe cooking environment

Clean cooking environment

If the cooking environment is not clean, there could be bacteria on the surfaces or equipment. This can cause food poisoning which can have fatal consequences.

Food poisoning affects thousands of people each year. Behind every toaster, in every fridge, on every chopping board, there might be germs. If they’re allowed to spread and multiply, the chances of food becoming contaminated increases. When contaminated food is eaten, it can cause vomiting, stomach cramps, diarrhoea and weakness.

The spread of bacteria can be prevented by sanitising tables/work surfaces with an anti-bacterial spray or hot soapy water and a clean cloth. Whilst cooking, staff should wash or wipe away any spills as soon as they happen. The work surfaces should also be disinfected after wiping up spills from raw meat/poultry/fish, eggs or unwashed fruit/vegetables.

Safe cooking environment

If the cooking environment is unsafe, accidents such as trips, falls and burns can happen. It is important that the cooking area is clear before any food preparation/cooking takes place. In particular, chairs, stools, bags, coats and other belongings must be stored away properly, so no-one can trip or fall over them. Also, any loose or trailing cables that people could trip over should be removed and floors should be checked for spillages so that there is no risk of slipping and falling.
